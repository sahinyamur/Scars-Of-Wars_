Saudi Arabia - Yemen War

PROGRESSION OF THE WAR

The Saudi Arabia-Yemen War (2015-2023) began as Saudi Arabia led a coalition to counter the Houthi rebels, who had taken over the Yemeni capital, Sana’a, and large parts of the country. The conflict emerged from Yemen's political instability following the Arab Spring, which had already left the nation deeply divided along sectarian and tribal lines. The Houthis, backed by Iran, aimed to overthrow the internationally recognized government of President Abdrabbuh Mansur Hadi.

The war quickly escalated into a regional proxy conflict, with Saudi Arabia and its allies conducting airstrikes and blockades, while the Houthis responded with ground offensives and missile strikes on Saudi territory. The humanitarian crisis intensified as food, medicine, and water supplies dwindled due to the blockade and ongoing violence, leading to what the United Nations described as one of the world's worst humanitarian catastrophes. Millions faced starvation, cholera outbreaks, and displacement.

As of 2023, the conflict remains unresolved, with sporadic ceasefires offering brief relief but no permanent peace. While Saudi Arabia has signaled a desire to de-escalate the conflict, the complex network of actors and grievances in Yemen poses significant challenges to achieving a sustainable resolution.
