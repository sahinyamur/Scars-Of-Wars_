Scars of Wars: Tracing Reconstruction and Social Transformation

Graduation Project Proposal by H. Şevik, Y. Şahin, and İ. Buralı


1. Introduction

Wars have been a defining factor in shaping the global political, economic, and social landscape. The impact of wars extends beyond immediate destruction and loss; they leave long- lasting effects on societies, economies, and the environment. From territorial changes to economic collapse and environmental degradation, the aftermath of wars continues to influence countries for generations. Since 1989, various major wars have not only redefined borders but also significantly altered the economic and social structures of nations, leaving scars that can be observed in data and geographical transformations.

This project focuses on analyzing the impacts of 16 significant wars from 1989 to the present day, utilizing data-driven methods to assess the geographical, economic, and environmental changes caused by these conflicts. By employing Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and advanced data analysis techniques, we aim to create a comprehensive and engaging online platform that will visually represent the changes these wars have inflicted. Through interactive maps, graphs, and data visualizations, this platform will illustrate the long-term effects of war on nations and help users grasp the complexities of how wars reshape societies.

Our project aims not only to present these changes but to foster a deeper understanding of the long-term consequences of wars and the importance of historical context in interpreting today's global realities. Through this, we hope to raise awareness about the ongoing effects of conflict, providing valuable insights into how historical wars continue to shape the present and future of affected regions.


1. Methodology


    1. Research and Data Collection:

      • Identification of Wars: Conduct thorough research to compile a list of 16 significant wars from 1989 onward, focusing on their historical context, causes, and consequences. This process will involve reviewing literature, historical texts, and documentaries to gain a comprehensive understanding of each conflict's background and impact.

      • Data Sources: Identify and evaluate reputable sources for data collection, including academic journals, government reports, NGO databases, and historical archives. Gather quantitative data, such as GDP, inflation rates, population statistics, and qualitative data,

        including personal testimonies, NGO reports, and government documents. This multifaceted approach aims to provide a holistic view of each war's impacts.


    2. Data Analysis:

      • Statistical Analysis: Utilize SQL for effective database management, R for advanced statistical analysis, and Python for data manipulation and processing. Analyze the collected data to identify trends and correlations, particularly in economic and environmental contexts. This analysis will help reveal patterns, such as the relationship between war and changes in GDP or population demographics over time.

      • GIS Visualization: Goggle Earth API to create detailed and interactive maps that showcase geographical changes resulting from wars. These visualizations will highlight population shifts, urban development, land use changes, and the physical destruction caused by conflict. By integrating spatial data with historical narratives, we aim to provide compelling evidence of the impacts of war on geographic landscapes.


    3. Website Development:

      • Design and User Experience: Use Figma to design an intuitive and visually appealing website layout, ensuring a user-friendly interface that allows easy navigation and comprehension of complex data. The design will focus on clarity and accessibility, making the information engaging for diverse audiences. Incorporate interactive features, such as clickable maps, infographics, and timelines, to enhance user engagement and facilitate exploration of the content.


      • Integration of Data: Develop the website using HTML, CSS, JS and JSON to connect the database with the website for real-time data interaction and visualization. Ensure that users can seamlessly explore different aspects of the wars and their impacts, including interactive filters that allow them to compare different wars or focus on specific geographical or economic data points.


    4. Feedback and Iteration:

      • Testing: Conduct user testing with a focus group comprising individuals from various backgrounds, including students, educators, and researchers, to gather feedback on the

        website’s functionality, design, and content. This process will involve observing how users

        interact with the site and identifying areas for improvement, ensuring that the platform meets user needs.

      • Refinement: Based on feedback gathered from user testing, make necessary adjustments to enhance usability, clarity, and engagement. This iterative process will involve refining the website's layout, improving navigation, and enhancing the overall user experience to ensure that the final product effectively communicates the project’s objectives and findings.


Conclusion

This project aims to provide a comprehensive analysis of the geographical, economic, and environmental impacts of significant wars from 1989 to the present. By utilizing a combination of data collection, statistical analysis, and GIS visualization, the project will not only illustrate the complexities of war's effects but also raise awareness about their lasting implications. The development of an interactive website will facilitate user engagement, making the findings accessible and understandable to a broader audience. Ultimately, this initiative seeks to foster a deeper understanding of the consequences of conflict, encouraging reflection on historical events and their relevance to contemporary society.
