Russia - Ukraine War

PROGRESSION OF THE WAR

The Russia-Ukraine War, which began in February 2022, is one of the most devastating conflicts in Europe since World War II. The war followed years of rising tensions, particularly after Russia’s 2014 annexation of Crimea and its support for separatists in eastern Ukraine. On February 24, 2022, Russia launched a full-scale invasion of Ukraine, citing security concerns and alleged threats to ethnic Russians in the Donbas region.

The war has been marked by intense fighting, with Russian forces initially targeting Kyiv and major cities, but facing strong Ukrainian resistance. Ukraine’s use of Western-supplied weapons, coupled with widespread international sanctions on Russia, has shaped the conflict into a prolonged and bloody stalemate in many areas. The war has caused significant civilian casualties and widespread destruction, with millions of Ukrainians displaced both internally and abroad.

Despite facing initial setbacks, Ukraine has managed to recapture some territories, supported by Western military and humanitarian aid. The conflict remains ongoing as of 2023, with no clear resolution in sight, and its effects are being felt globally, from economic disruptions to geopolitical realignments.
