Kosovo War

PROGRESSION OF THE WAR

The Kosovo War (1998-1999) was a conflict between Yugoslav forces, led by Slobodan Milošević, and the Kosovo Liberation Army (KLA), an ethnic Albanian separatist group. The war was fueled by longstanding ethnic tensions and repression of Kosovo’s Albanian majority by the Yugoslav government. Widespread atrocities, including massacres and forced displacement, prompted international condemnation.

NATO intervened in 1999, launching a bombing campaign against Yugoslav forces to stop the violence and protect civilians. The intervention forced Yugoslav forces to withdraw from Kosovo, which was placed under United Nations administration. However, the bombing campaign also caused significant civilian casualties and destruction, sparking debates about the ethics of military intervention.

Kosovo declared independence in 2008, though Serbia and several other countries have not recognized it. The war remains a sensitive issue, reflecting the deep divisions in the Balkans.
