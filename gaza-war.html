Gaza War

PROGRESSION OF THE WAR

The Gaza War (2008-2009), also known as Operation Cast Lead, was a three-week conflict between Israel and Hamas, the governing authority in Gaza. The war began after months of escalating rocket fire from Gaza into southern Israel and retaliatory Israeli airstrikes. Israel launched a ground invasion to neutralize Hamas’s military capabilities and stop the rocket attacks.

The war caused significant destruction in Gaza, with thousands of buildings damaged or destroyed. Civilian casualties were high, drawing widespread international condemnation of both sides. Israel was criticized for its heavy-handed tactics, while Hamas faced scrutiny for launching rockets from densely populated areas.

The conflict ended with a unilateral ceasefire declared by Israel, followed by a similar announcement by Hamas. However, the war did little to resolve the underlying issues, and periodic flare-ups in violence have continued in the years since.
