Nagorno-Karabakh War

PROGRESSION OF THE WAR

The Nagorno-Karabakh War of 2020 reignited a decades-long dispute between Armenia and Azerbaijan over the Nagorno-Karabakh region. The conflict began with Azerbaijani forces launching a military offensive to reclaim territories occupied by Armenian forces since the 1990s. The war featured intense fighting, including the use of drones, artillery, and modern military technology.

Azerbaijan, supported diplomatically and militarily by Turkey, made significant territorial gains, capturing key towns and strategic positions. Armenia, facing heavy losses, eventually agreed to a ceasefire brokered by Russia in November 2020. Under the agreement, Azerbaijan regained control of several districts surrounding Nagorno-Karabakh, while Russian peacekeepers were deployed to oversee the truce.

The war had profound social and political consequences for both countries, with thousands of casualties and widespread displacement. The conflict also underscored the fragility of peace in the South Caucasus region.
