China - India Border Conflicts

PROGRESSION OF THE WAR

The China-India Border Conflict (2020-2023) arose from disputes over the Line of Actual Control (LAC) in the Himalayan region. The conflict intensified in mid-2020 when clashes between Chinese and Indian troops in the Galwan Valley resulted in the deaths of 20 Indian soldiers and an undisclosed number of Chinese casualties. This was the first deadly encounter between the two nations in decades.

Both sides bolstered their military presence along the disputed border, leading to a prolonged standoff. Despite multiple rounds of diplomatic and military talks, tensions persisted, with occasional skirmishes and accusations of territorial intrusions. The conflict also had economic repercussions, with India banning several Chinese apps and restricting Chinese investments.

While some disengagement efforts have been made, the border remains a flashpoint, reflecting the broader strategic competition between the two Asian giants. The conflict has also highlighted the challenges of managing territorial disputes in an era of rising nationalism and geopolitical rivalry.
