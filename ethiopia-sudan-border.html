Ethiopia - Sudan Border Conflicts

PROGRESSION OF THE WAR

The Ethiopia-Sudan Border Conflicts (2020-2023) stemmed from long-standing disputes over the Al-Fashaga region, a fertile area claimed by both countries. The conflict escalated in late 2020 as Ethiopian forces became preoccupied with the Tigray War, allowing Sudanese forces to assert control over parts of the disputed territory. This led to skirmishes and casualties on both sides.

The situation was further complicated by the construction of Ethiopia's Grand Renaissance Dam (GERD) on the Blue Nile, which Sudan views as a threat to its water security. Negotiations over both the border and the GERD have been tense, with little progress made despite international mediation efforts.

The border conflict underscores the broader challenges of regional stability in the Horn of Africa, with both nations facing internal and external pressures. While full-scale war has been avoided, the lack of resolution keeps the risk of further clashes high.
