Current Gaza Conflict

PROGRESSION OF THE WAR

The Current Gaza Conflict (2012-2023) represents a prolonged period of hostilities between Israel and Palestinian factions, particularly Hamas. It began with Operation Pillar of Defense in 2012, followed by several major escalations, including the 2014 Gaza War and the 2021 conflict often referred to as the "May Escalation." The fighting has been marked by Israeli airstrikes, Hamas rocket attacks, and significant civilian casualties.

The humanitarian situation in Gaza has deteriorated due to the ongoing blockade imposed by Israel and Egypt, leaving much of the population dependent on international aid. Efforts to broker lasting peace have repeatedly failed, with both sides accusing each other of provocations and violations. The conflict has also deepened political divisions among Palestinians, with Hamas ruling Gaza and the Palestinian Authority governing parts of the West Bank.

As of 2023, sporadic escalations and ceasefires continue, with no resolution in sight. The international community remains divided, and the conflict remains a major flashpoint in the Middle East.
