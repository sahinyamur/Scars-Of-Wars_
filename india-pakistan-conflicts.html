India - Pakistan Border Conflicts

PROGRESSION OF THE WAR

The India-Pakistan Border Conflicts of 2019 were a result of heightened tensions between the two nuclear-armed neighbors, primarily over the disputed region of Jammu and Kashmir. The conflict escalated after a suicide bombing in Pulwama killed 40 Indian paramilitary personnel, an attack claimed by the Pakistan-based militant group Jaish-e-Mohammed. In retaliation, India conducted airstrikes in Balakot, marking the first such action across the Line of Control (LoC) since the 1971 war.

Pakistan responded by shooting down an Indian fighter jet and capturing its pilot, who was later released as a goodwill gesture. The skirmishes led to intense cross-border shelling and heightened global concerns over a possible full-scale war. International actors, including the United States and China, called for restraint from both sides to prevent further escalation.

Although tensions de-escalated after diplomatic interventions, the conflict deepened mistrust and hardened nationalist rhetoric on both sides. The events of 2019 continue to influence India-Pakistan relations, with little progress made toward resolving the Kashmir dispute.
